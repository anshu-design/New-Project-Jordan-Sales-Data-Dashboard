Project OverviewThis repository contains a comprehensive data analysis and visualization project focused on the Air Jordan resale market. The project leverages a specialized dataset from Kaggle to explore profit margins, pricing trends, and inventory health across various sales channels and shoe models.Key Features and InsightsBased on the project report, the dashboard provides the following analytical perspectives:Condition-Based Pricing Analysis: Demonstrates that "Deadstock" (brand new) shoes command the highest resale value, often selling for double or triple their original retail price.Platform Performance: Identifies Walk-in Retail and GOAT as the platforms yielding the highest profit margins compared to competitors like StockX.Model and Colorway Contribution: Highlights that Air Jordan 4 and Air Jordan 11 are the top contributors to total profit, with iconic colorways like "Chicago" and "Lost & Found" showing the highest demand.Inventory Velocity: Tracks the average days specific models stay in inventory, identifying which high-demand products move the fastest.Temporal Trends: Visualizes monthly profit fluctuations to identify seasonal peaks and hype-based demand cycles.Technical DetailsTools Used: Power BI for data structuring and visualization.Data Processing: Includes handling missing values, standardizing currency to USD, and data aggregation (sum/average).Dataset Attributes: Sales Channel, Shoe Condition, Model, Colorway, Retail/Resale Price, Profit Margin, and Days in Inventory.Future ScopePlanned enhancements for this project include:Integrating machine learning models to predict future resale prices and demand trends.Connecting real-time data APIs from platforms like StockX and GOAT.Developing automated recommendation systems for profitable resale investments.
